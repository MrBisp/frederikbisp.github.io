The role

I really love programming.

When I was in 8th grade I thought that I was going to be a programmer. But I also became really interested in the business side of things, and ended up working as a product manager while studying at Copenhagen Business School.

But I kept coming back to programming. I would code in my spare time. I would do data analysis in Python. I would code up prototypes to show the developers from my old work that our ideas could be implemented. Whenever I have the opportunity, I code - and time just flies by.

Not only do I love programming, I also like to build things that solves real problems. I like shaping ideas. I do not believe that there is such a thing as a perfect solution for anything. Because all solutions are a compromise in some way. But there is always a way to prioritize the most important things.

And I don't know why this is such a beautiful idea to me. But to me, there is just something profound about getting to the point, and building something that does what it should do - and nothing more.

I would love to join 37signals. I am sure I would be able to bring good energy and taste, while learning a lot from you.

The company

There is one reason, I would love to work for you specifically.

It's your philosophy. For several years now, I have been showing your products to my colleagues. I have been following your launches, listened to your podcasts, and read your books.

I have become fascinated by not only how you build products, but also how you build and run the company. You are questioniong the most foundational ideas such as "Why do we need to grow?".

Having worked at a "traditional startup", I have really started to believe that the way you run a company is just as important as the products you build. I am so tired of investors making questionable decisions and not thinking about what the customers actually need or want.

I would love to work for a company that understands that it doesn't have to be crazy at work. A company that is not afraid of saying what they believe in, especially when it is different from what other companies are doing.

Your way of working has proven successful for decades, and I would love to be part of that thoughtful, sustainable approach. I believe that it is in such an enviornment that I can grow and contribute the most as a person and as a professional.
